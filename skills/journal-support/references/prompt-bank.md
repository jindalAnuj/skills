# Prompt Bank

Use this file when the journal entry needs sharper questions, a better reframe, or more tailored action ideas.

## Better Questions

### When the user feels lost

- What feels unclear: the decision, the emotion, or the fear behind it?
- What are you pretending not to know already?
- If you stopped trying to look strong for a moment, what would you admit hurts?

### When the user feels ashamed

- What did you do, and what are you concluding about who you are?
- If a friend did the same thing, would you judge the person or the behavior?
- What repair is actually needed here, if any?

### When the user feels angry

- What boundary feels crossed?
- What need is the anger protecting?
- What conversation, request, or limit have you been postponing?

### When the user feels numb or empty

- When did you last feel engaged, relieved, or emotionally awake?
- Are you more likely shut down from exhaustion, disappointment, or fear?
- What have you stopped expecting from people or from yourself?

### When the user feels rejected

- What happened, and what meaning did you attach to it?
- What do you know for sure versus what are you inferring?
- What need for belonging, respect, or reassurance is active here?

## Reframe Patterns

### Identity to state

- "I am broken" -> "You are describing a painful state, not a fixed identity."
- "I am weak" -> "You may be overloaded, disappointed, or under-supported."
- "I am a failure" -> "A setback or bad pattern is not the whole person."

### Absolute to specific

- "Nothing works" -> "What has failed, and what has not been tried properly yet?"
- "Everyone leaves" -> "Who left, who stayed, and what patterns do you actually see?"
- "I always mess up" -> "Which repeated mistake are you pointing to?"

### Helplessness to agency

- "There is nothing I can do" -> "What is one action still available, even if it is small?"
- "I am stuck" -> "What part is stuck, and what part is still movable?"
- "It is too late" -> "What outcome is no longer available, and what outcome still is?"

## Action Ideas By State

### Overwhelmed

- Write the problem in one sentence.
- Pick the next physical action that takes under ten minutes.
- Remove one avoidable obligation from today.

### Ashamed

- Name the behavior without insulting yourself.
- Make one repair if repair is needed.
- Tell one trusted person the clean version of what happened.

### Angry

- Write the boundary that was crossed.
- Decide whether the next move is distance, a request, or a direct conversation.
- Delay any revenge impulse for one sleep cycle.

### Lonely

- Reach out to one person with a specific ask.
- Spend time in a setting where contact can happen naturally.
- Write what kind of connection you actually want instead of saying "people."

### Self-doubting

- List the evidence for the belief and the evidence against it.
- Replace the global judgment with a narrower statement.
- Choose one competence-building action instead of more rumination.

## Compact Output Template

Use this when the user wants a direct response:

1. "What I hear in this entry"
2. "What may be underneath it"
3. "Questions worth sitting with"
4. "What to do next"
5. "A more honest belief to test"
