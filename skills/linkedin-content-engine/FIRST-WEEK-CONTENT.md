# Your First Week of Content

**Strategy:** Educational content first. Build trust through competence. Show what AI can do. Make them think "I need to follow this person."

Personal stories come later (after 30+ days when they care about you).

"When X happens, do Y, then Z, then notify me" — running 24/7 without you.

The gap between "what AI can do" and "what most people use it for" is where the opportunity is.

## Post 3 (Wednesday) — The Agent Explainer

**Purpose:** Demystify "AI agents" — most people hear the term but don't understand it

---

"AI Agents" explained without the hype:

Regular AI: You ask a question → It gives an answer → Done.

AI Agent: You give it a goal → It figures out the steps → Takes actions → Adjusts when things don't work → Keeps going until done.

**Example:**

Regular AI: "Write me a cold email"
→ Gives you one email

AI Agent: "Research this company, find the right person to contact, write a personalized email based on their recent posts, and send it"
→ Does all of that automatically

The difference:
- Regular AI = Calculator (you do the work, it helps)
- AI Agent = Employee (you set the goal, it does the work)

We're moving from "AI as tool" to "AI as worker."

This changes everything about what's possible for small teams and solo operators.

A one-person business can now operate like a 10-person team.

What would you do with 10x your current capacity?

#AIAgents #Automation #FutureOfWork #Entrepreneurship

## Post 4 (Thursday) — The "What AI Can't Do" Post

**Purpose:** Build trust by being honest about limitations — not everything is hype

---

Let's be honest about what AI still can't do well:

❌ **Truly original creative direction**
It can execute your vision. It can't have vision.

❌ **Judgment calls that require context it doesn't have**
"Should we fire this client?" — AI doesn't know your relationship history.

❌ **Tasks requiring real-world physical action**
AI can plan your move. It can't carry the boxes.

❌ **Anything requiring genuine human trust**
People buy from people. AI can help, but closings need you.

❌ **Novel problem-solving in undefined spaces**
AI is great at "do what's been done before, faster." Less great at "invent something that doesn't exist."

**Here's the mental model:**

AI = Incredible execution on defined tasks
Human = Judgment, creativity, relationships, novel problems

The people winning right now aren't replacing themselves with AI.

They're pairing AI execution with human judgment.

That combination is unstoppable.

Where do you add value that AI can't?

#AI #HumanPlusAI #Leadership #FutureOfWork

## Week 2+ Content Themes

After the first week, rotate through these educational angles:

### Use Case Posts (Tuesday/Thursday)
Real examples of AI solving specific problems:
- "How a [profession] uses AI to [result]"
- "I built [thing] for [person]. Here's what happened."
- "Before AI vs After AI: [specific workflow]"

### Concept Explainers (Monday)
Demystify AI terms for non-technical people:
- "RAG explained without jargon"
- "What 'fine-tuning' actually means"
- "GPT vs Claude vs Gemini — when to use each"

### Capability Posts (Wednesday)
Show what's now possible:
- "5 things you can build in a weekend with AI"
- "This used to cost $50K. Now it costs $50."
- "What a solo founder can do in 2024 vs 2020"

### Limitation Posts (Friday)
Honest about what doesn't work yet:
- "Where AI still fails (and what to do instead)"
- "The AI hype vs reality checklist"
- "When NOT to use AI"

### Demo Posts (Any day)
Things you've actually built:
- "I built [X]. Here's the breakdown."
- "New automation I'm testing: [description]"
- "Client project showcase: [what + result]"

## Content Rules (Educational Phase)

### Do:
- Show concrete examples (not abstract concepts)
- Use before/after framing
- Give specific numbers (hours saved, cost, time to build)
- End with questions that make them imagine their own use case
- Be honest about limitations (builds trust)

### Don't:
- Talk about yourself or your journey (too early)
- Use jargon without explaining it
- Oversell ("AI will replace everything!")
- Be vague ("AI is powerful" — say HOW)

## Transition to Personal Content (After Day 30)

Once you have:
- 1,000+ impressions per post consistently
- Regular comments and engagement
- People recognizing your name

THEN introduce personal content:
- Your journey into AI
- Failures and lessons
- Behind-the-scenes of your work
- Opinions and predictions

**The sequence:**
1. "This person knows AI" (educational) — trust through competence
2. "I like this person" (personal) — trust through connection
3. "I want to work with this person" (conversion) — trust through relationship

Most people start at step 2. That's why they fail.

You earn the right to share your story by first proving you know what you're talking about.

## Measuring Success (Educational Phase)

**Good signs:**
- People asking questions in comments
- "I didn't know this was possible" responses
- Saves and shares (people finding it useful)
- DMs asking for more details

**Bad signs:**
- Only getting likes (no comments = not engaging)
- Engineers engaging but not your target audience
- No questions being asked

Adjust based on what gets engagement from the RIGHT people (non-technical folks wanting things built).
