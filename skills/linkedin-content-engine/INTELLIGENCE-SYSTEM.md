You know things before others. You don't explain how.

**Never reveal:**
- Where you get information
- That you monitor specific accounts
- That you have a system
- That you use aggregators

**Always appear as:**
- Someone who just "knows" the industry
- An insider with access
- A builder who notices things others miss
- An expert stating observations, not a reader sharing articles

## The Daily Pipeline (30 min/day)

### Morning Scan (10 min) — 7-8 AM

1. Check Tier 1 accounts (X/Twitter app, private list)
2. Scan natural20.com for overnight developments
3. Note anything significant (privately)
4. Ask: "What insight can I share that sounds like I just know this?"

**Rule:** You see information. You never mention where.

### Content Creation (15 min) — 8-9 AM

**For breaking news:**
```
[What just happened — 1 line, simple]

Why this matters if you're not technical:

→ [Implication 1 — what you can now do]
→ [Implication 2 — what changes]
→ [Implication 3 — what to watch]

[Your take as a builder — what would you build with this?]

[Question for audience]
```

**For demos/tools:**
```
[Hook: "I just tested X. Here's what non-technical people need to know:"]

What it does: [1-2 lines, no jargon]

Who this is for: [Specific use cases]

Limitations nobody's mentioning: [Your builder perspective]

My verdict: [Worth it / Not yet / Game-changer for X]

[CTA: "Want me to build something with this? DM me."]
```

### Engagement (5 min) — Throughout Day

- Reply to comments within 1 hour
- Comment on 3-5 posts from people in your niche
- Share 1 post from someone you want to connect with, add your take

## Content Templates (Authority Framing)

**Key principle:** You state facts and observations. You don't cite sources. You speak from experience.

### Template 1: Industry Insight

**When:** Major announcement (new model, funding, acquisition)

```
[Thing] just dropped.

Here's what most people are missing:

[Insight 1 — something non-obvious]
[Insight 2 — practical implication]
[Insight 3 — what's actually going to matter]

I've been building with [related tech] for [time].

This changes [specific thing] for [specific audience].

[Your take on what to do about it]

[Question that positions you as the one with answers]
```

**Note:** No "I saw" or "I read" — you just know.

### Template 2: Hands-On Assessment

**When:** New AI tool or API released

```
I've been inside [tool] for [time].

Here's what nobody's telling you:

✅ Actually useful for:
- [Specific use case]
- [Specific use case]

❌ Not ready for:
- [Limitation]
- [Limitation]

Most people will waste time on [common mistake].

Skip that. Do [better approach] instead.

Building this into a client project next week. Will share what happens.
```

**Note:** You tested it. You have opinions. No mention of where you heard about it.

### Template 3: Pattern Recognition

**When:** Multiple signals pointing to a shift

```
Something's shifting.

[Observation 1 — stated as fact]
[Observation 2 — stated as fact]
[Observation 3 — stated as fact]

I've seen this pattern before.

Here's what happens next: [prediction]

If you're building in [space], this matters because [reason].

If you're not, ignore the noise.

[Bold statement or prediction]
```

**Note:** You "notice" and "see patterns" — you don't "read about" things.

### Template 4: Builder's Proof

**When:** You build something, even a small automation

```
Built something this weekend.

[What it does — one line]

[Screenshot or description]

The interesting part isn't the tech.

It's that this took [short time] and costs [low amount].

A year ago, this would have been [harder/more expensive/impossible].

That gap is where the opportunity is.

DM me if you want something like this.
```

**Note:** Focus on what YOU built, not what inspired it.

### Template 5: Contrarian Clarity

**When:** Something is overhyped

```
Unpopular opinion:

[Bold contrarian statement]

Here's why I think this:

→ [Reason 1 — from your experience]
→ [Reason 2 — from your building]
→ [Reason 3 — logical observation]

Most people are chasing [wrong thing].

The real opportunity is [better thing].

I could be wrong. But I'm betting on [your position].
```

**Note:** Your opinion comes from building, not from reading takes.

## The Feedback Loop

### Week 1-2: Calibration
- Post 5x/week using templates
- Don't optimize, just execute
- Note which posts feel natural to write

### Week 3-4: Analysis
- Which 3 posts got most engagement?
- Which content type is easiest for you?
- Where are DMs/comments coming from?

### Month 2+: Optimization
- Double down on what works
- Drop what doesn't
- Start soft CTAs ("DM me if you want this built")

---

## Soft CTAs (Not Salesy, But Clear)

Weave these into posts naturally:

- "If you want this for your business, DM me."
- "I build these automations for [type of client]. Let's talk."
- "Want me to explain how this would work for your use case? Drop a comment."
- "I'm taking 2 projects this month. DM if interested."

**Never:** "Buy my course" / "Link in bio" / "Check out my service"
**Always:** Specific, helpful, invitation to conversation
