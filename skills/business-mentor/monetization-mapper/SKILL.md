# Monetization Mapper

Turn attention into revenue. This skill helps you design monetization that matches your audience and content type.

## When to Use

- You have traffic/followers but no revenue
- You're choosing between revenue models (ads vs subscriptions vs affiliates)
- You want to optimize monetization of existing product
- You're building content/audience and want to monetize from day one

## The Monetization Hierarchy

Revenue quality from highest to lowest:

```
1. Product Revenue      → Sell your own product/service
2. Recurring Revenue    → Subscriptions, memberships
3. Transaction Revenue  → Per-sale, usage-based
4. Affiliate Revenue    → Commission on others' products
5. Sponsorship Revenue  → Brand deals, sponsored content
6. Ad Revenue           → CPM, display ads (lowest quality)
```

**Rule:** Always aim for the highest tier your audience supports.

### Step 2: Content-Monetization Matrix

Match your content type to optimal monetization:

| Content Type | Best Monetization | Why |
|--------------|-------------------|-----|
| **Educational** | Courses, templates, consulting | High intent, willing to pay for results |
| **Entertainment** | Merch, brand deals, fan support | Emotional connection, low direct value |
| **Utility/Tools** | Freemium, subscription | Clear value, measurable ROI |
| **Lifestyle/Inspiration** | Affiliates, brand deals | Audience trusts recommendations |
| **B2B/Professional** | SaaS, services, high-ticket | High willingness to pay |
| **Niche Hobby** | Digital products, community | Passionate, price-insensitive |

## Monetization Playbooks

### Playbook A: Content Creator → Product

*Example: Instagram page with 50K followers*

```
Week 1-2: Identify top pain point from DMs/comments
Week 3-4: Create digital product (template, guide, mini-course)
Week 5: Soft launch to engaged followers (stories, DMs)
Week 6: Public launch with content promotion
Week 7+: Iterate based on feedback, build product ladder
```

**Target:** $5-50 product, 2-5% of followers convert = $5K-125K

### Playbook B: Traffic → Affiliate Revenue

*Example: Blog or tool with organic traffic*

```
Step 1: Identify what your audience already buys
Step 2: Find affiliate programs (start with Amazon, expand to niche)
Step 3: Create comparison content (best X for Y)
Step 4: Add contextual recommendations in existing content
Step 5: Track and optimize (which products convert?)
```

**Target:** $1-100 per conversion, optimize for high-ticket affiliates

### Playbook C: Tool → Freemium SaaS

*Example: Free utility/app with users*

```
Step 1: Identify power users (high usage)
Step 2: Define premium features they'd pay for
Step 3: Implement usage limits on free tier
Step 4: Add payment (Stripe, Lemon Squeezy)
Step 5: Grandfather existing users or migrate gradually
```

**Target:** 2-5% free-to-paid conversion

### Playbook D: Audience → Community/Membership

*Example: Engaged niche following*

```
Step 1: Create private community (Discord, Circle, Skool)
Step 2: Offer exclusive content/access
Step 3: Price at $10-50/month (higher for B2B)
Step 4: Launch to warmest followers first
Step 5: Use community as feedback loop for products
```

**Target:** 1-3% of audience converts to membership

## Avoiding Monetization Traps

### Trap 1: Ad-Only Thinking

❌ "I'll grow big and monetize with ads"
✅ "I'll monetize from day one with [specific model]"

**Math reality:**
- YouTube ads: $2-5 per 1,000 views
- Need 1M views/month for ~$3K
- Same audience buying $20 product at 1% = $200K

### Trap 2: Vanity Metrics

❌ Celebrating followers, views, likes
✅ Track: email subscribers, product waitlist, revenue

**Reframe:**
- 10K followers with 0 revenue = hobby
- 500 email subscribers buying = business

### Trap 3: Audience-Product Mismatch

❌ Selling courses to entertainment audience
✅ Match monetization to WHY they follow

**Example (your Instagram case):**
- Cute cats/babies audience → merch, brand deals (entertainment)
- Dental promotion page → affiliate, lead gen (utility)
- Relationship advice → digital products, coaching (education)

### Trap 4: Racing to Free

❌ "I'll make it free to get users first"
✅ Charge from day one, even if small

**Why:** Free users ≠ customers. Validation requires payment.

## High-Ticket Monetization Angles

When audience is small but engaged, go high-ticket:

| Model | Price Range | Requirements |
|-------|-------------|--------------|
| Consulting/Coaching | $100-500/hour | Expertise, scheduling |
| Done-for-you service | $1K-10K | Delivery capacity |
| Cohort course | $500-2K | Community, live element |
| Productized service | $500-5K/month | Process, results |

**Rule:** 10 customers × $1,000 = 1,000 customers × $10
