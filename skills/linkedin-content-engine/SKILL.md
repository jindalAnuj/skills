# LinkedIn Content Engine

End-to-end system for creating LinkedIn content that gets reach, builds authority, and drives business. Designed for software engineers who want to build a personal brand and monetize their expertise.

## How LinkedIn Actually Works (So You Don't Have To Learn This)

These rules are baked into every piece of content this skill generates. You don't need to memorize them — the system applies them automatically.

### The Algorithm (What Gets Reach)

1. **First 60 minutes are everything.** LinkedIn measures early engagement velocity. Posts that get comments in the first hour get pushed to more feeds.
2. **Dwell time matters.** LinkedIn tracks how long people pause on your post. Longer posts that people actually read beat short posts with quick scrolls.
3. **Comments > Reactions > Shares.** Comments are 10x more valuable than likes. Posts that spark conversation win.
4. **Consistency compounds.** Posting 3-5x/week for 90 days beats posting daily for 2 weeks then disappearing.
5. **Text-only posts outperform links.** LinkedIn suppresses external links (they want people staying on platform). Images perform well. Carousels (PDF uploads) get highest reach.
6. **Your network seeds your reach.** First 500 connections see your post. If they engage, it expands to 2nd/3rd degree. Quality connections > quantity.
7. **Posting time matters less than you think.** But Tuesday-Thursday 7-9 AM local time of your target audience is the sweet spot.

### What Gets Ignored (Common Mistakes)

- Generic motivational content ("Never give up!" — nobody engages)
- Sharing links with no context (algorithm suppresses, looks lazy)
- Posting about yourself without making it about the reader
- Writing like a corporate press release
- Using hashtags excessively (3-5 relevant ones max)
- Posting irregularly (algorithm punishes inconsistency)

## Step 1: Positioning (Run Once, Update Monthly)

Before generating any content, establish positioning. Ask the user these questions:

### Questions to Ask

1. **What's your specific technical expertise?**
   - Not "software engineer" — what specifically? (e.g., "I build high-scale backend systems in Go", "I've shipped 12 React Native apps", "I've led engineering teams of 20+")

2. **Who do you want to reach?**
   - Other engineers? (for hiring/community)
   - Non-technical founders? (for consulting/agency)
   - Product managers? (for cross-functional influence)
   - Aspiring developers? (for courses/education)
   - CTOs/VPs? (for enterprise sales)

3. **What's the business angle?**
   - Consulting/freelancing (content → inbound leads)
   - SaaS (content → product awareness)
   - Courses/info products (content → email list → sales)
   - Hiring/recruiting (content → talent pipeline)
   - Not sure yet (content → audience → figure it out)

4. **What's your unfair advantage?**
   - What do you know that most people in your audience don't?
   - What mistakes have you made that others are about to make?
   - What contrarian opinion do you hold that you can defend?

5. **Time commitment?**
   - Minimal (15-30 min/day): AI generates, you review and post
   - Moderate (30-60 min/day): AI generates, you tweak and engage with comments
   - Serious (1-2 hrs/day): Full content + commenting on others' posts

### Generate Positioning Statement

From the answers, generate:

```
I help [TARGET AUDIENCE] [ACHIEVE WHAT] by sharing [YOUR UNIQUE ANGLE].
```

Examples:
- "I help non-technical founders avoid costly engineering mistakes by sharing lessons from 10 years of shipping software."
- "I help mid-level developers become senior engineers by breaking down system design concepts with real-world examples."
- "I help startups build MVPs faster by sharing practical architecture patterns that scale."

**Store this positioning. Every post must align with it.**

## Step 2: Content Pillars (Run Once, Update Monthly)

Based on positioning, define 4-5 content pillars. These are the recurring themes all content falls under.

### Content Phases (Critical Sequencing)

**Phase 1: Educational (Days 1-30)**
Build trust through competence. Show what AI can do. Prove you know your stuff.

**Phase 2: Personal (Days 30+)**
Once they trust your expertise, share your journey and opinions.

**Why this order matters:** Nobody cares about your story until they believe you're worth listening to. Competence first, connection second.

---

### Pillars for Phase 1 (Educational)

1. **AI Capabilities** — "X things AI can do that most people don't realize"
2. **Use Cases** — "I built [thing] for [person]. Here's what happened."
3. **Concept Explainers** — Demystify AI terms without jargon
4. **Honest Limitations** — What AI can't do (builds trust through honesty)
5. **Demos** — Things you've actually built, with specifics

### Pillar Distribution Per Week (5 posts/week) — Phase 1

| Day | Pillar | Why |
|-----|--------|-----|
| Monday | Concept Explainer | Start week with value, educational |
| Tuesday | Use Case | Concrete example, shows capability |
| Wednesday | Capability/Demo | Mind-blow moment, high save rate |
| Thursday | Use Case | Another concrete example |
| Friday | Honest Limitations | Trust-building through honesty |

### Pillars for Phase 2 (Personal — After Day 30)

1. **Lessons Learned** — Failures, mistakes, what you'd do differently
2. **Industry Takes** — Opinions on trends (contrarian = better)
3. **Behind the Scenes** — What building actually looks like
4. **Predictions** — Where things are going
5. **Journey/Business** — Your path, your decisions

Start mixing personal content at 20% of posts after Day 30, increasing gradually.

## Step 3: Content Generation

### The Generation Workflow

When the user wants content, follow this process:

1. **Get the raw input** from the user. This can be:
   - A story or experience ("Today I debugged a production issue that...")
   - An opinion ("I think microservices are overused because...")
   - A lesson ("I learned the hard way that code reviews should...")
   - A concept to explain ("I want to talk about database indexing")
   - Just a topic ("Write something about API design")

2. **Select the best format** (see formats below)

3. **Write the post** applying all LinkedIn optimization rules

4. **Generate 3 variations** with different hooks

5. **Recommend posting time and hashtags**

### Content Formats (Ranked by Reach)

#### Format 1: The Listicle (Highest Save Rate)
```
[Hook: surprising statement or number]

Here's what I learned after [X experience]:

1. [Point] — [1-2 line explanation]
2. [Point] — [1-2 line explanation]
...
7-10 points

[Closing question or call to action]
```

**When to use:** Technical tips, career advice, tool recommendations  
**Example hook:** "I mass hired 47 engineers in 2 years. Here are 9 hiring red flags I now catch in the first 5 minutes:"

#### Format 2: The Use Case (Highest Credibility)
```
[Hook: Problem someone had]

[Who they were — 1 line]

[The problem — specific, relatable]

[What we built — simple explanation]

[How it works — 3-4 bullet points]

[The result — specific numbers]

[What this means for the reader]

[Question to the audience]
```

**When to use:** Showing AI solving real problems, building credibility, demonstrating capability  
**Example hook:** "A coach asked me: 'Can AI help me respond to DMs faster?' Here's what we built in 3 hours:"

**Note:** Story format (personal failures, lessons) works better AFTER you've built trust through educational content. Save stories for Phase 2 (Day 30+).

#### Format 3: The Contrarian Take (Highest Engagement)
```
[Hook: "Everyone says X. I disagree."]

[Why the common wisdom is wrong — 2-3 points]

[What's actually true — your argument]

[Evidence or experience backing your position]

[Invite debate: "Am I wrong? Tell me why."]
```

**When to use:** Industry opinions, technology debates, process challenges  
**Example hook:** "Stop writing unit tests. Yes, I'm serious. Here's why 90% of unit tests are a waste of your team's time:"

#### Format 4: The Framework (Highest Share Rate)
```
[Hook: "How I [achieved result] using this simple framework:"]

[Name the framework — give it a memorable name]

Step 1: [Action] — [Why it works]
Step 2: [Action] — [Why it works]  
Step 3: [Action] — [Why it works]

[Show result or outcome]

[Offer to share template/details in comments]
```

**When to use:** Processes, methodologies, decision-making approaches  
**Example hook:** "I use a 3-question test before every architecture decision. It's saved me from 6 rewrites this year:"

#### Format 5: The Comparison (High Dwell Time)
```
[Hook: "X vs Y — here's what nobody tells you:"]

[Common perception]

[Reality based on your experience]

[When to use each]

[The nuanced answer most people miss]
```

**When to use:** Technology comparisons, career stages, approach trade-offs  
**Example hook:** "Junior devs write code. Senior devs delete code. Here's the difference nobody explains:"

#### Format 6: The Carousel (Highest Reach per Impression)
```
Slide 1: [Bold hook title — 5-8 words max]
Slide 2: [The problem — why this matters]
Slides 3-8: [One point per slide — visual, concise]
Slide 9: [Summary / key takeaway]
Slide 10: [CTA — follow for more, save this, share your take]
```

**When to use:** Complex topics, tutorials, visual concepts  
**Note:** Generate as a PDF or use a design tool. Text-heavy slides work fine on LinkedIn — don't over-design.

#### Format 7: The Explainer (Highest Trust-Building)
```
[Term/concept] explained without the hype:

[Simple definition — 1-2 lines]

[Analogy that makes it click]

[Example of how it works in practice]

[What this means for non-technical people]

[When to care about this vs when to ignore it]
```

**When to use:** Demystifying AI terms, building credibility with non-technical audience  
**Example hook:** "'AI Agents' explained without the jargon:"

#### Format 8: The Honest Limitations (Highest Trust)
```
Let's be honest about what AI [still can't do / gets wrong / overpromises]:

❌ [Limitation 1 — with brief explanation]
❌ [Limitation 2 — with brief explanation]
❌ [Limitation 3 — with brief explanation]

[Why this matters]

[What to do instead / the real answer]

[Balanced perspective that shows expertise]
```

**When to use:** Building trust by not overselling, differentiating from hype accounts  
**Example hook:** "Let's be honest about what AI still can't do well:"

## Step 4: Writing Rules (Applied Automatically)

Every post generated by this skill follows these rules:

### Hook Rules (First 2-3 Lines)
- Must create curiosity or tension (the "see more" click)
- Use numbers, surprising statements, or direct address
- Never start with "I'm excited to announce" or "Thrilled to share"
- Never start with a question (questions as hooks have low click rates)
- Front-load the most interesting part

### Structure Rules
- Short paragraphs (1-3 lines max)
- Use line breaks aggressively — dense text gets scrolled past
- One idea per paragraph
- Use "→" or "—" or numbered lists for scannability
- Total length: 150-300 words for text posts (sweet spot for dwell time)

### Voice Rules
- Write like you're talking to one specific person, not an audience
- Use "you" more than "I"
- Be specific — numbers, names, timeframes (not "a lot of users" → "47,000 users")
- Show vulnerability — admitting mistakes builds trust
- Have a point of view — lukewarm opinions get zero engagement
- No corporate jargon, no buzzword soup

### Authority Rules (CRITICAL)
**Never reveal sources.** You are the authority, not a curator.

**Never say:**
- "I saw on Twitter..."
- "I read that..."
- "According to [source]..."
- "[Person] said..."
- "There's an article about..."

**Always say:**
- "I've been noticing..."
- "Here's what's actually happening..."
- "I've been building with..."
- "Pattern I'm seeing..."
- "In my experience..."

**The goal:** People should wonder "how does this person always know what's happening?" — never "oh they just read Twitter"

You consume information privately. You output original-sounding insight publicly. The pipeline is invisible.

### Closing Rules
- End with a question (drives comments)
- Or end with a strong opinion (drives debate)
- Never end with "What do you think?" alone — too generic
- Better: "Have you seen this in your team? What's your version of this?"

### Hashtag Rules
- 3-5 hashtags max, placed at the end
- Mix broad (#SoftwareEngineering) with niche (#SystemDesign)
- Never use #Motivation #Success #Hustle (wrong audience)
- Research which hashtags your target audience follows

2. **Format**: Which format template

## Step 6: Engagement Strategy (Tell User What to Do)

Content alone isn't enough. Provide these engagement instructions:

### Daily Engagement Routine (20-30 min)

1. **Before posting** (10 min): Comment thoughtfully on 5 posts from people in your niche. Not "Great post!" — add value, share perspective, ask a real question.
2. **After posting** (5 min): Reply to every comment on your post within the first hour. Speed matters for algorithm.
3. **Evening** (10 min): Check notifications, reply to any new comments, thank people who shared.

### Engagement Tactics

- **Comment first, post second.** Being visible in others' comment sections gets you followers.
- **Reply to comments with questions.** This creates threads, which boost your post's reach.
- **Tag people mentioned in your post** (but only if relevant and you have a relationship).
- **Reshare your best posts** after 3-4 weeks. Most of your audience didn't see them the first time.

## Step 7: Performance Tracking

After 2 weeks, ask the user for their metrics:

### Metrics That Matter

| Metric | What It Means | Target (first 90 days) |
|--------|--------------|----------------------|
| Impressions | How many feeds your post appeared in | 1,000+ per post |
| Engagement rate | (Reactions + Comments) / Impressions | 3-5% |
| Profile views | People clicking to learn about you | 100+/week |
| Connection requests | People wanting to follow your work | 20+/week |
| DMs / Inbound | People reaching out for business | 2-5/month |

### Metrics That Don't Matter

- Follower count (vanity metric without engagement)
- Likes without comments (passive engagement, low value)
- Viral posts with wrong audience (engineers liking your post when you target founders)

### Optimization Loop

Every 2 weeks:
1. What 3 posts got the most engagement? → Do more of that format/topic
2. What 3 posts flopped? → Analyze why (bad hook? wrong audience? boring topic?)
3. Which content pillar performs best? → Increase its frequency
4. Where are profile views coming from? → Double down on that channel
