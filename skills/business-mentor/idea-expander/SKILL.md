# Idea Expander

Once something works, expand it efficiently. This skill helps you scale with minimal additional effort.

## When to Use

- You have a working product generating some revenue
- You want to reuse your tech stack for new products
- You're looking for adjacent opportunities
- You want to compound your existing success

## Expansion Strategies

## Expansion Decision Framework

Before expanding, answer:

```markdown
## Expansion Evaluation

**Expansion idea:** [Description]

### Fit Assessment

1. Does this use existing tech? [Yes/Partially/No]
2. Does this serve existing customers? [Yes/Partially/No]
3. Can I launch in <2 weeks? [Yes/No]
4. Does this compound existing efforts? [Yes/No]
5. Will this cannibalize current product? [Yes/No]

### Resource Check

- Engineering time needed: [hours]
- Marketing effort needed: [Low/Medium/High]
- Support burden increase: [Low/Medium/High]

### Expected Outcome

- Additional MRR in 3 months: $___
- Confidence level: [High/Medium/Low]

### Decision

- [ ] **PROCEED** - High fit, reasonable resources, good ROI
- [ ] **DEFER** - Good idea, but not now
- [ ] **REJECT** - Doesn't fit expansion criteria
```

## Creative Expansion Techniques

### The "What Else Do They Buy?" Method

1. Look at your best customers
2. What other tools do they use?
3. Can you build a simpler/better version?
4. Can you integrate with those tools?

### The "Keyword Adjacent" Method

1. Find keywords your product ranks for
2. Look for related searches
3. Build products for those searches
4. Cross-link everything

### The "Pain Audit" Method

1. Review customer support tickets
2. What do people ask for that you don't do?
3. Build the top 3 requests as paid features or separate products

### The "Competitor Gap" Method

1. List all competitor features
2. What do they NOT do well?
3. Build specialized tools for those gaps
