# Weekly Accountability

Your weekly check-in to stay focused, measure progress, and combat the drift that kills most side projects.

## When to Use

- Every week (ideally same day/time)
- When you feel like switching projects
- When motivation is low
- When you're unsure what to work on next
- When you haven't made progress in a while

## Accountability Rules

### The 8-Week Rule

**Do not abandon a project before 8 weeks of consistent effort.**

- Week 1-2: Build MVP
- Week 3-4: Launch to small audience
- Week 5-6: Iterate based on feedback
- Week 7-8: Marketing push

Only after week 8, with data, can you make an informed decision.

### The Switch Criteria

You may ONLY switch projects if:

1. [ ] You've given it 8+ weeks of consistent work
2. [ ] Revenue trajectory is clearly wrong (not growing for 4+ weeks)
3. [ ] New idea scores 1.5+ points higher in idea-evaluator
4. [ ] You've documented learnings from current project
5. [ ] You're not switching because it got hard

### The Commitment Contract

Write this out and revisit when tempted:

```
I, [Name], commit to working on [Project] for [8-12] weeks.

I will not switch projects unless I meet ALL switch criteria.
I will complete my weekly reviews every [Day].
I will be honest with myself about progress.

When I want to give up, I will remember that most successes
come after the moment when I wanted to quit.

Signed: ___
Date: ___
```

## When You're Stuck

### Progress Audit

If you're not making progress, diagnose:

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Fix |
|---------|--------------|-----|
| Not shipping | Perfectionism | Ship ugly, iterate |
| No users | Not marketing | Spend 50% time on marketing |
| No revenue | Wrong pricing or ICP | Use monetization-mapper |
| No motivation | Wrong project or burnout | Honest assessment needed |
| Scattered effort | Too many projects | Kill extras, focus on one |

### Motivation Maintenance

When motivation dips:

1. **Reconnect with why** - Why did you start this?
2. **Find small wins** - What can you ship today?
3. **Talk to a user** - Hear how you're helping
4. **Review progress** - You've come further than you think
5. **Reduce scope** - Smaller goal, easier win

### The Hard Truth Questions

Answer these when you want to quit:

1. Have I actually tried, or just thought about trying?
2. Am I quitting because it's hard, or because it's wrong?
3. What would I tell a friend in this situation?
4. What will I regret more: quitting or persisting?
5. Is the new thing actually better, or just shinier?
