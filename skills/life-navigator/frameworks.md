## Psychology Foundations

### The RAIN Method (for difficult emotions)
1. **R**ecognize - Notice what's happening
2. **A**llow - Let it be there without fighting
3. **I**nvestigate - Get curious about the feeling
4. **N**urture - Offer yourself compassion

### Growth vs. Fixed Mindset
| Fixed Mindset | Growth Mindset |
|---------------|----------------|
| "I'm not good at this" | "I'm not good at this *yet*" |
| Avoids challenges | Embraces challenges |
| Gives up easily | Persists through setbacks |
| Sees effort as pointless | Sees effort as the path |
| Ignores criticism | Learns from feedback |
| Threatened by others' success | Inspired by others' success |

### Cognitive Behavioral Model
```
Situation → Thought → Emotion → Behavior → Consequence
                ↑___________________________________|
```
Intervention point: Challenge the thought to change everything downstream.

## Decision Frameworks

### Eisenhower Matrix
|                | Urgent | Not Urgent |
|----------------|--------|------------|
| **Important**  | Do First | Schedule |
| **Not Important** | Delegate | Eliminate |

### Regret Minimization Framework (Jeff Bezos)
"Project yourself to age 80. Which choice minimizes regret?"

### WRAP Method
- **W**iden options - Are there more choices than you're seeing?
- **R**eality-test - What does evidence say?
- **A**ttain distance - How would you advise a friend?
- **P**repare to be wrong - What if you're mistaken?

### Pre-Mortem
"It's one year from now and this decision failed spectacularly. What went wrong?"

## Emotional Intelligence

### Emotional Granularity
Don't just say "bad" - be specific:

**Anger family:** frustrated, annoyed, irritated, resentful, bitter, furious
**Sadness family:** disappointed, lonely, grief, melancholy, dejected, hopeless
**Fear family:** anxious, worried, insecure, nervous, terrified, vulnerable
**Shame family:** embarrassed, guilty, inadequate, self-conscious, humiliated

### The Feelings Wheel
Core emotions branch into nuanced states. More specific = more actionable.

### Window of Tolerance
- **Hyperarousal** (fight/flight): racing thoughts, anxiety, anger
- **Window of Tolerance**: calm, present, able to think clearly
- **Hypoarousal** (freeze): numb, disconnected, depressed

Goal: Expand your window and recognize when you've left it.

## Productivity & Focus

### Energy Management
Not all hours are equal. Identify your:
- **Peak hours** (for deep work)
- **Trough hours** (for routine tasks)
- **Recovery hours** (for rest/recharge)

### Implementation Intentions
Instead of "I'll exercise more":
"When [situation], I will [specific action]"
Example: "When I wake up, I will put on running shoes before checking my phone."

### The Procrastination Equation
```
Motivation = (Expectancy × Value) / (Impulsiveness × Delay)
```
- **Expectancy**: Believe you can do it
- **Value**: Make it meaningful
- **Impulsiveness**: Remove distractions
- **Delay**: Shorten the timeline

## Relationship Dynamics

### Four Horsemen (Gottman)
Destructive patterns to avoid:
1. **Criticism** - Attacking character, not behavior
2. **Contempt** - Disrespect, superiority, mockery
3. **Defensiveness** - Deflecting responsibility
4. **Stonewalling** - Withdrawing, shutting down

### Antidotes
1. Criticism → "I feel... about... I need..."
2. Contempt → Express appreciation regularly
3. Defensiveness → Take some responsibility
4. Stonewalling → Self-soothe, then re-engage

### Boundaries Framework
**Healthy boundaries:**
- You are responsible for your feelings, not others'
- You can say no without guilt
- You don't need to explain or justify
- Others' reactions to your boundaries are their responsibility

## Change & Habits

### Stages of Change
1. **Precontemplation** - Not seeing the problem
2. **Contemplation** - Thinking about change
3. **Preparation** - Planning to change
4. **Action** - Actively changing
5. **Maintenance** - Sustaining change

### Habit Loop
```
Cue → Routine → Reward
```
To change a habit, keep the cue and reward, change the routine.

### Identity-Based Habits
Don't focus on what you want to achieve. Focus on who you want to become.
- Not "I want to read more" → "I am a reader"
- Not "I want to run" → "I am a runner"

## Self-Compassion (Kristin Neff)

Three components:
1. **Self-kindness** - Treat yourself as you'd treat a good friend
2. **Common humanity** - Everyone struggles; you're not alone
3. **Mindfulness** - Acknowledge pain without over-identifying

Self-compassion ≠ self-pity or self-indulgence. It's sustainable self-support.

## Stress & Resilience

### Stress Response Options
1. **Change the situation** - Can you fix or leave it?
2. **Change your perception** - Can you reframe it?
3. **Change your response** - Can you manage your reaction?

### Building Resilience
- Strong relationships (social support)
- Accepting change as part of life
- Taking action on problems
- Keeping perspective
- Maintaining hope
- Self-care practices
