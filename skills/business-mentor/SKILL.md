# Business Mentor System

## The $1M Path

**Goal:** $83,333 MRR ($1M ARR)
**Timeline:** 6 months
**Method:** Systematic execution with accountability

## Skill Package Overview

| Skill | Purpose | When to Use |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| **idea-evaluator** | Score ideas before committing | New idea, comparing opportunities |
| **market-research** | Validate market and customers | Before building anything |

| **marketing-strategy** | Plan go-to-market | Ready to launch or grow |
| **weekly-accountability** | Stay focused, track progress | Every week |
| **idea-expander** | Scale what's working | Have traction, want more |

## Quick Start Guide

### Week 1: Foundation

1. **Set up tracking**
   - Create a spreadsheet or Notion for your $1M journey
   - Schedule weekly review time (same day/time each week)
   - Write your commitment contract (see weekly-accountability)

2. **Evaluate your current ideas**
   - Use idea-evaluator on your top 3 ideas
   - Pick the highest-scoring idea

3. **Do market research**
   - Complete market-research for chosen idea
   - Validate problem exists
   - Size the opportunity

### Week 2-3: Validation

4. **Design monetization**
   - Use monetization-mapper
   - Know exactly how you'll make money before building

5. **Plan marketing**
   - Use marketing-strategy to choose channels
   - Don't start marketing yet, just plan

### Week 4-6: Build

6. **Build MVP**
   - Minimum viable product
   - Ship ugly, ship fast
   - Get something in front of users

### Week 7-8: Launch

7. **Execute marketing plan**
   - Follow your marketing-strategy playbook
   - Measure everything

### Week 9+: Iterate & Scale

8. **Weekly accountability**
   - Complete weekly reviews every week
   - Stay honest about progress

9. **Expand what works**
   - When you have traction, use idea-expander
   - Portfolio, platforms, or verticals

## The Core Principles

### 1. Score Before You Build
Never commit to an idea without running idea-evaluator. Gut feelings lie.

### 2. Validate Before You Code
Market research is cheaper than building the wrong thing.

### 3. Monetize From Day One
Design revenue model before building. Free is not a strategy.

### 4. Focus Beats Variety
One focused project beats ten scattered attempts.

### 5. Compound Effort
Every product should build on the last. Reuse tech, reuse learnings.

### 6. Data Over Feelings
Track metrics. Make decisions based on numbers, not emotions.

### 7. Consistency Over Intensity
Weekly reviews every week. Slow and steady wins.

## Getting Help

When you're stuck:

1. **State your situation clearly**
   - Current stage (idea/building/launched/growing)
   - Current metrics (users, revenue)
   - Specific challenge

2. **Ask for the right skill**
   - "Help me evaluate this idea" → idea-evaluator
   - "Help me understand this market" → market-research
   - "Help me figure out how to make money" → monetization-mapper
   - "Help me get customers" → marketing-strategy
   - "Help me stay focused" → weekly-accountability
   - "Help me scale this" → idea-expander

3. **Be honest about progress**
   - The skills work better with accurate information
   - Don't hide failures—learn from them

## Emergency Protocols

### When Motivation Dies

1. Complete a weekly-accountability review
2. Look at your wins (you have them)
3. Reduce scope—what's the smallest next step?
4. Talk to a customer—remember why you started

### When You Want to Quit

1. Is this project fundamentally broken, or just hard?
2. Have you given it 8 weeks of real effort?
3. What would you tell a friend in this situation?
4. Re-read your commitment contract

### When a Shiny Object Appears

1. Do NOT start working on it
2. Run it through idea-evaluator
3. Compare score to current project
4. Only switch if new idea scores 1.5+ higher AND current project isn't working
